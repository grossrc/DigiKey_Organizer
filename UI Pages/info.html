What is this?

A locally hosted inventory system for DigiKey parts, running on this Raspberry Pi. It stores item data and stock levels in PostgreSQL. The scanner and database are tailored for DigiKey’s Data Matrix labels.

Why local? Fast, private, and always available on your network. Browse the full catalog at http://lab-parts.local/catalog (might not work on mobile devices).

How it works

  • Scan a DigiKey part (Data Matrix) with Scan-In.
  • Confirm or adjust the quantity (e.g., if some were used before storing).
  • Assign a bin/location (e.g., R3-B12).
  • This stores the part info, attributes, electrical characteristics, categories, and location in the database.
  • Use the catalog website to plan projects and avoid double-buying.
  • Checkout those parts when you’re ready.

What the catalog provides

  • All the stocked parts that have been logged
  • An "Add to List" feature allowing users to shop around stocked parts
  • CSV download of a desired part list
  • QR code-based automated checkout (recommended)
  • Search by category, attribute, or checkout the product/datasheet links
  • Data stored in PostgreSQL (structure available in repo)

Quick start

A) Stock parts (Scan-In)

  1. Tap Scan-In on the home screen.
  2. Scan the DigiKey label (Data Matrix).
  3. Set the quantity you’re stocking (adjust if some were used).
  4. Input the bin/location where you’ll physically place it.
  5. Save- the part, attributes, and location are automatically stored in the database.
Be consistent with bin naming (e.g., R3-B12) so search and checkout stay reliable. Improper or careless storage will cause long-term issues.

B) Plan & choose parts (Catalog)

  1. On a computer, open http://lab-parts.local/catalog.
  2. Browse available stock and add to your list as you go.
  3. When your list is complete, choose either:
    • Generate QR code for automated checkout, or
    • Download CSV of selected parts.
Recommended: QR checkout reduces errors and keeps the database clean.

Checkout — QR (recommended)

  1. From the catalog, generate your QR code.
  2. Scan the QR code when prompted.
  3. Follow the guided steps; confirm items and then remove the entire package.
  4. Done! Items are removed from stock. Use them for your project.

Checkout — Manual

  1. Scroll to find items
  2. Confirm location carefully.
  3. Checkout and repeat.
Parts are tracked at the package level, so the entire package should be checkout out even if you only need a few of the stocked amount. Example: if there are 20 resistors but you only need 5. Checkout all 20, then check back in the 15 remaining.

Accuracy matters

This system is the last line of defense. Wrong bin, inaccurate quantity, or checking out digitally without physically moving parts (or vice versa) will create significant issues and may require a reset. You can reset the database (i.e. clear stock) from the 'Database' button on the homepage.
  • Always double-check the bin/location when scanning in.
  • Keep physical and digital actions in sync.
  • Use the QR flow whenever possible.
  • If you spot a mismatch, fix it immediately (checkout item(s) and re-enter into the system).
